Θ(n²) Tight Bound Visualization

f(n) = 4n² + 8n + 12  ·  c₁ = 3, c₂ = 5, n₀ = 10
f(n) = 4n² + 8n + 12
c₁·n² = 3n²  (lower bound)
c₂·n² = 5n²  (upper bound)
n₀ = 10  (threshold)
Zoom:
✗  Before n₀ = 10

f(n) exceeds the upper bound 5n². The extra terms 8n + 12 are still large enough to push f(n) above c₂·n². The sandwich does not yet hold — hover n < 10 to see this.

✓  After n₀ = 10

f(n) is permanently sandwiched inside the green band between 3n² and 5n². As n grows, n² dominates and the lower-order terms become negligible — exactly what Θ notation captures.

Formal statement:  ∃ c₁ = 3, c₂ = 5, n₀ = 10 such that
3n²  ≤  4n² + 8n + 12  ≤  5n²   for all n ≥ 10
∴  f(n) = Θ(n²)
